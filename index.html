Support

Help & questions.

Short answers to the things people ask most. Anything else, drop me a line.

QHow do I set the scale?

Use the Calibrate menu (top-left). Enter the drawing’s stated ratio, draw a line along a known dimension and type its real length, or pick a standard scale from the list. It works in metric and imperial.

QWhere are my measurements saved?

Inside the PDF itself. The file stays an ordinary PDF that opens anywhere, and reopens in Cactus with your measurements intact.

QCan I export to Excel?

Yes — a categorised summary as an .xlsx workbook, a flat annotated PDF of your marked-up drawing, or plain CSV.

QDoes Cactus need an account or internet?

No. Cactus runs entirely on your Mac. Your drawings never leave your machine and no data is collected.

QMy measurements look the wrong size.

It’s almost always the scale. Re-issued and re-plotted PDFs often don’t match their stated ratio — calibrate from a dimension you can verify on the drawing. Each page is calibrated independently.

QMy drawing won’t open.

Cactus will open true PDF files. If a file is actually an image (PNG or JPEG) renamed to .pdf, restore its real extension — Cactus needs a real PDF.

QCan I use Cactus on another Mac?

Yes. Re-download it from the App Store on any Mac signed in to the same Apple ID — no licence keys to move. Your measurements travel inside each PDF, so opening a saved file brings everything with it.
