Frequently asked

How do I set the scale?
Use the Calibrate menu (top-left). Either enter the drawing's stated ratio, draw a line along a known dimension and type its real length, or pick a standard scale from the list. Works in metric and imperial.
Where are my measurements saved?
Inside the PDF itself. The file stays an ordinary PDF that opens anywhere, and reopens in Cactus with your measurements intact.
Can I export to Excel?
Yes — export a categorised summary as a spreadsheet, plus a flat annotated PDF of your marked-up drawing.
Does Cactus need an internet connection or account?
No. Cactus runs entirely on your Mac. Your drawings never leave your machine and no data is collected.
My drawing won't open.
Cactus opens PDF files. If a file is actually an image (PNG or JPEG) renamed to .pdf, rename it back to its real extension and it will open in other apps. Cactus needs a true PDF.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Cactus: PDF Takeoff ("the app") is a Mac application that runs entirely on your device. Your privacy is simple to describe because the app is built not to collect anything.

Data we collect: none. The app does not collect, transmit, store remotely, sell, or share any personal data or usage data. There is no account, no sign-in, and no analytics or tracking of any kind.

Your documents: the PDF drawings you open and the measurements you make stay on your Mac. They are never uploaded to us or to any third party. Measurements are saved inside your own PDF files, on your own machine.

Network use: the app does not require an internet connection to function and does not send your data anywhere.

Third parties: the app contains no third-party advertising, no third-party analytics, and no embedded tracking SDKs.

Contact: for any privacy questions, email sebcarley@outlook.com.
